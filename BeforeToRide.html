Here are the tips for before riding a Bike

Cycling is a sport that is particed in a open field, advancing with the bicicle is possible thanks to the work of the rider and the bicycle itself. As in all sports, there is a correct way to practice the sport, for example you cannot play soccer with a deflated ball or playing golf in the street; Likewise, in cycling, there are guidelines that will help you make your outings better and you can enjoy more on the bike, here we present them.

1.Be Cautious:

Caution is the most important thing, always be attentive to traffic regulations, respect the signs; On the roads and trails, be aware of obstacles, animals and cars may be present during the ride.

Remember that you are not alone or on the road.

2.Try to go out with someone:

Going out alone is good, many people do it but it is not highly recommended, especially if you are learning so far, you do not want to get lost in the middle of nowhere without help to repair a puncture.

Also with friends everything is better.

3.Use a Tracker:

If for the previous point you do not find anyone, it is normal, not everyone will be able to be available every day. So the best option is to use a tracker or a distance measurement and mapping service, we recommend Strava, since it is the most used mobile application among cyclists. more than 55 million users and 3 billion activities uploaded by the community. Definitely one of the best options.

(Click on the image to go to the official strava site)

4.Use a Cycling-Computer:

Using a cycle computer is great for reviewing data such as pedaling frequency, watts of power, your average and maximum speed , elapsed time, or hour. You will have all these details in the easiest way possible, just by looking at the cycling computer on the handlebars of your bike.

There are various brands of cycling computers, such as Garmin, Polar, Car Eye, SRM Power and many more.

5.Keep hydrated

Being hydrated is one of the most important points in any sport, but in cycling it is even more important, it is estimated that a cyclist should drink water every 15 minutes, do not be thirsty, you must hydrate, for a ride of several kilometers it is quite necessary replenish energy, in addition to energy expenditure, added to dehydration and fatigue can lead to health problems.

There are different ways to carry water on the bicycle, with the cans and supports to carry them in the frame of the bike, to the camelback style backpacks to carry the water on the back and be taken by a hose.
